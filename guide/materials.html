Starter Kit — Everything You Need to Begin

Here's a concrete shopping list with links to buy each item. The idea is to start with affordable but functional gear, then upgrade as you grow.

Press & Clamping

Item Est. Cost Where to Buy Why
VEVOR Book Press 12" $90-130 Amazon — search "VEVOR book press" Best budget book press. Cast iron, adjustable pressure. Enough for most Bibles. This is your single most important tool investment.
Nipping press (used) $50-150 eBay / antique stores Faster than a book press for quick nipping. Keep an eye out for used ones.
Binder clips (2" size) $5-10 for set Amazon / office supply store Holding text blocks during gluing. Cheap and essential.
Magnet strips / heavy weights $10-20 Amazon / hardware store Holding pages flat during edge work. Weights can be anything heavy.

Starter Checklist — Order of Priorities

If you're buying in stages, here's the priority order:

  1. Essential — $150-200: VEVOR book press, bone folder, X-Acto knife + blades, cutting mat, metal ruler, Jade 403 PVA, glue brushes, awl, linen thread, needles, beeswax, wax paper.
  2. Sewing — $50-80: Sewing frame (DIY or buy), stabbing awl, Berisford ribbons, headbands, sandpaper pack, gold acrylic paint (for practice edges).
  3. Materials — $50-100: Calfskin leather, Davey Board, bookbinding cloth, base Bible (buy one to practice on).
  4. Finishing — $80-200: Entry-level foil stamping machine, type set, foil rolls, skiving knife, sharpening stone.
  5. Gilding — $80-200: Gold leaf, gilding size, agate burnisher, gilding brush, gilding cushion, gilding knife (buy this set when you're ready for premium edge work).

Estimated full startup tool investment: $400-600 for functional (able to complete a quality rebind) · $1,200-2,000 for full setup including stamping and gilding.
